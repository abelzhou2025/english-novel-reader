AI Business Strategy AI in Action Deep Dives Human-AI Relationships Retail \& Logistics AI World of Work

www.artificialintelligence-news.com Dashveenjit Kaur

www.artificialintelligence-news.com Dashveenjit Kaur

Walmart's December 9 transfer to Nasdaq wasn't just a symbolic gesture. The US$905 billion retailer is making its boldest claim yet: that it's no longer a traditional discount chain, but a tech-powered enterprise using AI to fundamentally rewire its retail operations.

沃尔玛于 12 月 9 日转至纳斯达克不仅仅是一个象征性的举动。这家市值 9,050 亿美元的零售商做出了迄今为止最大胆的声明：它不再是传统的折扣连锁店，而是一家利用人工智能从根本上重塑其零售业务的科技型企业。

But beyond the marketing spin and the parade of AI announcements, what's genuinely transforming at the world's largest retailer -- and where are the gaps between ambition and execution?

但除了营销宣传和人工智能公告的游行之外，这家全球最大的零售商真正发生了什么变化——雄心和执行力之间的差距在哪里？

The Agentic AI pivot: Purpose-built, not off-the-shelf

Walmart's AI strategy diverges sharply from competitors chasing generic large language models. According to CTO Hari Vasudev, the company is deploying what it calls "purpose-built agentic AI" -- specialised tools trained on Walmart's proprietary retail data rather than one-size-fits-all solutions.

沃尔玛的人工智能战略与追求通用大语言模型的竞争对手截然不同。据首席技术官 Hari Vasudev 称，该公司正在部署所谓的“专用代理人工智能”——根据沃尔玛专有零售数据进行训练的专门工具，而不是一刀切的解决方案。

"Our approach to agentic AI at Walmart is surgical," Vasudev wrote in a May 2025 blog post. "Extensive early testing proved that, for us, agents work best when deployed for highly specific tasks, to produce outputs that can then be stitched together to orchestrate and solve complex workflows."

Vasudev 在 2025 年 5 月的博客文章中写道：“我们在沃尔玛采用代理人工智能的方法是外科手术。”“广泛的早期测试证明，对我们来说，代理在部署用于高度特定的任务时效果最好，产生的输出可以拼接在一起以编排和解决复杂的工作流程。”

This translates to tangible applications, the company says: Walmart's "Trend-to-Product" system cuts fashion production timelines by 18 weeks. Its GenAI Customer Support Assistant now routes and resolves issues autonomously without human intervention.

该公司表示，这可以转化为切实的应用：沃尔玛的“趋势到产品”系统将时装生产时间缩短了 18 周。其 GenAI 客户支持助理现在可以自主路由和解决问题，无需人工干预。

Developer productivity tools handle test generation and error resolution in CI/CD pipelines. Meanwhile, the company's retail-specific LLM "Wallaby" -- trained on decades of Walmart transaction data -- powers everything from item comparison to personalised shopping journey completion.

开发人员生产力工具可处理 CI/CD 管道中的测试生成和错误解决。与此同时，该公司针对零售业的法学硕士“Wallaby”接受了数十年沃尔玛交易数据的培训，为从商品比较到个性化购物旅程完成的一切提供支持。

The infrastructure underpinning this? Element, Walmart's proprietary MLOps platform, designed to avoid vendor lock-in and optimise GPU use in multiple cloud providers. It's an in-house "factory" that gives Walmart speed and flexibility competitors wrestling with third-party platforms can't match.

支撑这个的基础设施？Element 是沃尔玛专有的 MLOps 平台，旨在避免供应商锁定并优化多个云提供商中的 GPU 使用。这是一家内部“工厂”，为沃尔玛提供了与第三方平台角力的竞争对手无法比拟的速度和灵活性。

Real numbers: Where AI delivers measurable impact

Walmart has been unusually transparent about specific ROI metrics, offering a rare glimpse into enterprise AI economics:

沃尔玛对于具体的投资回报率指标异常透明，让我们得以难得一睹企业人工智能经济学的风采：

Data operations: GenAI improved over 850 million product catalogue data points -- a task that would have required 100 times the headcount using manual processes, according to CEO Doug McMillon's August 2024 earnings call.

数据运营：根据首席执行官 Doug McMillon 在 2024 年 8 月的财报电话会议上的说法，GenAI 改进了超过 8.5 亿个产品目录数据点，如果使用手动流程，这项任务需要 100 倍的员工人数。

Supply chain efficiency: AI-powered route optimisation eliminated 30 million unnecessary delivery miles and avoided 94 million pounds (42,000 tons) of CO2 emissions. The company won the prestigious Franz Edelman Award in 2023 for this technology -- and has since commercialised it as a SaaS product for other businesses.

供应链效率：人工智能驱动的路线优化消除了 3,000 万不必要的配送里程，并避免了 9,400 万磅（42,000 吨）二氧化碳排放。该公司凭借这项技术于 2023 年赢得了著名的 Franz Edelman 奖，并已将其商业化为其他企业的 SaaS 产品。

Store operations:Digital twin technology predicts refrigeration failures up to two weeks in advance, auto-generating work orders complete with visual models, wiring diagrams, and required parts. Sam's Club's AI-powered exit technology has reduced member checkout times by 21%, with over 64% of members now using the friction-free system in all locations.

商店运营：数字孪生技术最多可提前两周预测制冷故障，自动生成包含视觉模型、接线图和所需零件的工作订单。山姆会员店的人工智能退出技术已将会员结账时间缩短了 21%，目前超过 64% 的会员在所有地点都使用无摩擦系统。

Customer experience: Dynamic delivery algorithms analyse traffic patterns, weather conditions, and order complexity to predict delivery times down to the minute, enabling 17-minute express deliveries in markets the company has tested.

客户体验：动态配送算法分析交通模式、天气状况和订单复杂性，以精确到分钟的方式预测配送时间，从而在公司测试过的市场中实现 17 分钟快递配送。

The human cost: "AI will change every job"

McMillon hasn't sugarcoated the workforce implications. Speaking at a Bentonville workforce conference in September 2025, he said: "It's very clear that AI is going to change literally every job. Maybe there's a job in the world that AI won't change, but I haven't thought of it."

麦克米伦并没有粉饰对劳动力的影响。他在 2025 年 9 月的本顿维尔劳动力大会上发表讲话时表示：“很明显，人工智能将改变几乎所有工作。也许世界上有一种工作是人工智能不会改变的，但我没有想到这一点。”

But Walmart positions this as a transformation not an elimination. McMillon expects total headcount to remain flat even as revenue grows -- meaning jobs will shift, not disappear. White-collar roles face the earliest disruption through chatbots handling customer service and supply chain tracking, while store and warehouse workers will eventually see tasks absorbed by autonomous systems.

但沃尔玛将其定位为转型而不是消除。麦克米伦预计，即使收入增长，总员工人数也将保持不变，这意味着工作岗位将会转移，而不是消失。白领角色最早面临由处理客户服务和供应链跟踪的聊天机器人带来的干扰，而商店和仓库工作人员最终将看到自动化系统吸收任务。

The company is investing heavily in re-skilling programmes. "We've got to create the opportunity for everybody to make it to the other side," McMillon said at the Bentonville conference. Automation equipment operator Chance at Walmart's Palestine, Texas, distribution centre described the shift: "It used to be 85% physical. Now it's 85% mental. I'm solving problems with my mind, not just my body."

该公司正在大力投资再培训计划。“我们必须为每个人创造机会到达彼岸，”麦克米伦在本顿维尔会议上说。德克萨斯州巴勒斯坦沃尔玛配送中心的自动化设备操作员 Chance 描述了这种转变：“过去 85% 是体力劳动。现在 85% 是精神劳动。我用我的思想解决问题，而不仅仅是用我的身体。”

The Nasdaq gambit: Repositioning for tech valuations

Walmart's exchange transfer was explicitly framed around its AI transformation. CFO John David Rainey stated the move reflects the company "setting a new standard for omnichannel retail by integrating automation and AI."

沃尔玛的交易转移明确围绕其人工智能转型进行。首席财务官 John David Rainey 表示，此举反映了该公司“通过集成自动化和人工智能，为全渠道零售树立了新标准”。

The subtext? Walmart wants the valuation multiples tech companies command. At a P/E ratio of 40.3x -- higher than Amazon and Microsoft -- the market is partially buying the transformation story. Potential inclusion in the tech-heavy Nasdaq 100 index would drive passive fund investment regardless of AI execution.

潜台词？沃尔玛希望获得科技公司的估值倍数。市盈率为 40.3 倍（高于亚马逊和微软），市场部分接受了这一转型故事。无论人工智能的执行情况如何，被纳入以科技股为主的纳斯达克 100 指数的可能性都将推动被动基金投资。

Analysts are split on whether the premium is justified. Jefferies' Corey Tarlowe argued the move signals Walmart is "less of a traditional retail corporation and more of a technology firm." But sceptics note the company still derives revenue from razor-thin retail margins, not high-margin software or cloud services -- despite commercialising tools like Route Optimisation.

分析师对于溢价是否合理存在分歧。杰富瑞 (Jefferies) 的科里·塔洛 (Corey Tarlowe) 认为，此举表明沃尔玛“不再是一家传统零售公司，而是一家科技公司”。但怀疑者指出，尽管将路线优化等工具商业化，但该公司的收入仍然来自微薄的零售利润，而不是高利润的软件或云服务。

Verdict: Genuine transformation with execution risk

Walmart's AI strategy is neither pure hype nor guaranteed success. The company is making structural investments in proprietary infrastructure, deploying AI at genuine scale with published operational benefits, and acknowledging workforce implications most enterprises dodge.

沃尔玛的人工智能战略既不是纯粹的炒作，也不是成功的保证。该公司正在对专有基础设施进行结构性投资，大规模部署人工智能并公布运营效益，并承认大多数企业回避的劳动力影响。

But significant execution risks remain: managing fragmented agent ecosystems, preventing algorithmic bias at scale, competing against external shopping agents, and determining appropriate automation boundaries while maintaining accuracy.

但重大的执行风险仍然存在：管理分散的代理生态系统、防止大规模算法偏差、与外部购物代理竞争，以及在保持准确性的同时确定适当的自动化边界。

The company's candidness about challenges -- "often, a co-pilot model, with humans and AI working as a team, is the most effective approach" -- suggests leadership understands AI isn't a silver bullet.

该公司对挑战的坦诚态度——“通常，人类和人工智能作为一个团队合作的副驾驶模式是最有效的方法”——表明领导层明白人工智能并不是灵丹妙药。

For enterprises watching Walmart's playbook, the lesson is to build for specificity, not generality. Invest in proprietary data moats, plan for workforce transformation, not just cost reduction, and recognise that even with massive resources and technical talent, agentic AI remains early-stage technology with genuine limitations.

对于关注沃尔玛策略的企业来说，教训是要针对具体情况而不是通用性进行构建。投资专有数据护城河，规划劳动力转型，而不仅仅是降低成本，并认识到即使拥有大量资源和技术人才，代理人工智能仍然是具有真正局限性的早期技术。

The question isn't whether Walmart is using AI -- it demonstrably is. It's whether this surgical, infrastructure-heavy approach delivers sustainable competitive advantage, or if the company is automating itself into the same low-margin trap with shinier tools.

问题不在于沃尔玛是否正在使用人工智能——它显然正在使用人工智能。问题在于这种外科手术式的、基础设施密集的方法是否能带来可持续的竞争优势，或者该公司是否正在利用更出色的工具自动陷入同样的​​低利润陷阱。

That answer won't be clear for several years -- but Walmart's willingness to bet its US$905 billion in market cap on the transformation suggests leadership believes the former.

这个答案在几年内都不会明确——但沃尔玛愿意将其 9050 亿美元的市值押在转型上，这表明领导层相信前者。
