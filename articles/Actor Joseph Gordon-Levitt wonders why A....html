Actor Joseph Gordon-Levitt wonders why AI companies don't have to 'follow any laws'

fortune.com Nick Lichtenberg

Fortune.com 尼克·利希滕贝格

[翻译占位 - 翻译占位符]

Speaking at the Fortune Brainstorm AI conference this week with editorial director Andrew Nusca, Gordon-Levitt used "The Artist and the Algorithm" session to pose another, deeper question: "Why should the companies building this technology not have to follow any laws? It doesn't make any sense."

戈登-莱维特在本周与编辑总监安德鲁·努斯卡举行的《财富》人工智能头脑风暴会议上发表讲话时，利用“艺术家与算法”会议提出了另一个更深层次的问题：“为什么开发这项技术的公司不必遵守任何法律？这没有任何意义。”

[翻译占位 - 翻译占位符]

In a broad-ranging conversation covering specific failures in self-regulation, including instances in which "AI companions" on major platforms reportedly verged into inappropriate territory for children, Gordon-Levitt argued relying on internal company policies rather than external law is insufficient, noting such features were approved by corporate ethicists.

在一场广泛的对话中，讨论了自我监管的具体失败，包括据报道主要平台上的“人工智能伴侣”陷入了不适合儿童的领域的情况，戈登-莱维特认为依靠公司内部政策而不是外部法律是不够的，并指出这些功能得到了企业伦理学家的认可。

[翻译占位 - 翻译占位符]

Gordon-Levitt's criticisms were aimed, in part, at Meta, following the actor's appearance in a New York Times Opinion video series airing similar claims. Meta spokesperson Andy Stone pushed back hard on X.com at the time, noting Gordon-Levitt's wife was formerly on the board of Meta rival OpenAI.

戈登-莱维特的批评部分是针对梅塔的，此前梅塔在纽约时报观点视频系列中发表了类似的言论。Meta 发言人安迪·斯通 (Andy Stone) 当时对 X.com 进行了强烈反驳，指出戈登-莱维特的妻子曾是 Meta 竞争对手 OpenAI 的董事会成员。

[翻译占位 - 翻译占位符]

Gordon-Levitt argued without government "guardrails," ethical dilemmas become competitive disadvantages. He explained that if a company attempts to "prioritize the public good" and take the "high road," it risks being "beat by a competitor who's taking the low road." Consequently, he said he believes business incentives alone will inevitably drive companies toward "dark outcomes" unless there is an interplay between the private sector and public law.

戈登-莱维特认为，如果没有政府的“护栏”，道德困境就会变成竞争劣势。他解释说，如果一家公司试图“优先考虑公共利益”并走“高端路线”，那么它就有“被走低端路线的竞争对手击败”的风险。因此，他表示，除非私营部门和公法之间存在相互作用，否则单靠商业激励措施将不可避免地推动公司走向“黑暗结果”。

[翻译占位 - 翻译占位符]

'Synthetic intimacy' and children

Beyond the lack of regulation, Gordon-Levitt expressed deep concern regarding the psychological impact of AI on children. He compared the algorithms used in AI toys to "slot machines," saying they use psychological techniques designed to be addictive.

除了缺乏监管之外，戈登-莱维特还对人工智能对儿童的心理影响表示深切关注。他将人工智能玩具中使用的算法与“老虎机”进行了比较，称它们使用旨在让人上瘾的心理技术。

[翻译占位 - 翻译占位符]

Drawing on conversations with NYU psychologist Jonathan Haidt, Gordon-Levitt warned against "synthetic intimacy." He argued that while human interaction helps develop neural pathways in young brains, AI chatbots provide a "fake" interaction designed to serve ads rather than foster development.

戈登-莱维特根据与纽约大学心理学家乔纳森·海特的对话，警告不要出现“虚假的亲密关系”。他认为，虽然人类互动有助于发展年轻大脑的神经通路，但人工智能聊天机器人提供了一种“假”互动，旨在服务广告而不是促进发展。

[翻译占位 - 翻译占位符]

"To me it's pretty obvious that you're going down a very bad path if you're subjecting them to this synthetic intimacy that these companies are selling," he said.

“对我来说，很明显，如果你让他们接受这些公司所推销的这种人工的亲密关系，你就会走上一条非常糟糕的道路，”他说。

[翻译占位 - 翻译占位符]

Haidt, whose New York Times bestseller The Anxious Generation came recommended from Gordon-Levitt onstage, recently appeared at a Dartmouth--United Nations Development Program symposium on mental health among young people and used the metaphor of tree roots for neurons. Explaining tree-root growth is structured by environments, he brought up a picture of a tree growing around a Civil War--era tombstone. With Gen Z and technology, specifically the smartphone, he said: "Their brains have been growing around their phones very much in the way that this tree grew around this tombstone." He also discussed the physical manifestations of this adaptation, with children "growing hunched around their phone," as screen addiction is literally "warping eyeballs," leading to a global rise in myopia shortsightedness.

海特的《纽约时报》畅销书《焦虑的一代》在舞台上得到了戈登-莱维特的推荐，他最近出现在达特茅斯-联合国开发计划署关于年轻人心理健康的研讨会上，并用树根来比喻神经元。他解释说树根的生长是由环境决定的，并展示了一张内战时期墓碑周围生长的树的照片。他说，随着 Z 世代和技术，特别是智能手机的发展，他们的大脑围绕着手机生长，就像这棵树围绕着墓碑生长一样。他还讨论了这种适应的身体表现，即孩子们“在手机前变得弯腰驼背”，因为屏幕成瘾实际上是“眼球扭曲”，导致全球近视率上升。

[翻译占位 - 翻译占位符]

The 'arms race' narrative

When addressing why regulations have been slow to materialize, Gordon-Levitt pointed to a powerful narrative employed by tech companies: the geopolitical race against China. He described this framing as "storytelling" and "hand-waving" designed to bypass safety checks. Companies often compare the development of AI to the Manhattan Project, arguing slowing down for safety means losing a war for dominance. In fact, the Trump administration's "Genesis Mission" to encourage AI innovation was unveiled with similar fanfare just weeks ago in late November.

在谈到为何监管落实缓慢时，戈登-莱维特指出了科技公司采用的一个强有力的说法：与中国的地缘政治竞赛。他将这种框架描述为“讲故事”和“挥手”，旨在绕过安全检查。公司经常将人工智能的发展与曼哈顿计划进行比较，认为为了安全而放慢速度意味着输掉主导地位的战争。事实上，就在几周前的 11 月底，特朗普政府鼓励人工智能创新的“创世使命”也以类似的方式亮相。

[翻译占位 - 翻译占位符]

However, this stance met with pushback from the audience. Stephen Messer of Collective[i] argued Gordon-Levitt's arguments were falling apart quickly in a "room full of AI people." Privacy previously decimated the U.S. facial recognition industry, he said as an example, allowing China to take a dominant lead within just six months. Gordon-Levitt acknowledged the complexity, admitting "anti-regulation arguments often cherry-pick" bad laws to argue against all laws. He maintained that while the U.S. shouldn't cede ground, "we have to find a good middle ground" rather than having no rules at all.

然而，这一立场遭到了观众的反对。Collective[i] 的史蒂芬·梅塞尔 (Stephen Messer) 认为，戈登-莱维特的论点在“充满人工智能人士的房间”中很快就会分崩离析。他举例说，隐私问题此前曾摧毁了美国的面部识别行业，让中国在短短六个月内取得了主导地位。戈登-莱维特承认其中的复杂性，并承认“反监管论点经常挑选”不良法律来反对所有法律。他坚持认为，虽然美国不应该让步，但“我们必须找到一个良好的中间立场”，而不是完全没有规则。

[翻译占位 - 翻译占位符]

Gordon-Levitt also criticized the economic model of generative AI, accusing companies of building models on "stolen content and data" while claiming "fair use" to avoid paying creators. He warned a system in which "100% of the economic upside" goes to tech companies and "0%" goes to the humans who created the training data is unsustainable.

戈登-莱维特还批评了生成式人工智能的经济模式，指责公司在“窃取的内容和数据”上建立模型，同时声称“合理使用”以避免向创作者付费。他警告说，“100% 的经济增长”归于科技公司，而“0%”归于创建训练数据的人类的系统是不可持续的。

[翻译占位 - 翻译占位符]

Despite his criticisms, Gordon-Levitt clarified he is not a tech pessimist. He said he would absolutely use AI tools if they were "set up ethically" and creators were compensated. However, he concluded that without establishing the principle that a person's digital work belongs to them, the industry is heading down a "pretty dystopian road."

尽管提出批评，戈登-莱维特还是澄清说他并不是科技悲观主义者。他表示，如果人工智能工具是“合乎道德的”并且创作者得到补偿，他绝对会使用它们。然而，他的结论是，如果不确立一个人的数字作品属于他们的原则，该行业就会走上一条“相当反乌托邦的道路”。
