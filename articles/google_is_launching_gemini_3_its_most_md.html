Google is launching Gemini 3, its 'most intelligent' AI model yet

正在翻译标题...
The internet. Mobile devices. The cloud. Each of these technologies changed our perspective on the world of work: how we do it, where we do it, and what tools we use.
正在翻译...
And now we have generative AI, with all its potential to change the very nature of work itself. Already, it's put new demands on the infrastructure of work, just like those other technologies before it.
正在翻译...
Take the networks carrying digital traffic between enterprises, data centers, applications, and devices: Nine out of 10 businesses and other organizations (91 percent) are boosting their investments in computer networks to handle AI workloads, according to new research from Cisco. Nearly two-thirds (71 percent) report that AI's demands are too much for the data center capacity they currently have. That's why 88 percent are working to increase capacity wherever they can, whether it's on-site or in the cloud.
正在翻译...
Exactly how AI will change our jobs is still an open question, but so far, fears that it's about to outright replace humans at work seem to be overblown. A recent study out of the University of Chicago and the University of Copenhagen found no impact by generative AI on hours worked or earnings across the 11 professions it studied. "Our findings challenge narratives of imminent labor market transformation due to Generative AI," the researchers conclude.
正在翻译...
Instead, the AI era seems to be about finding ways to augment human capacity with AI, with the potential to bring out the best in both.
正在翻译...
"AI can do a lot of human-like work at machine speeds, but it can't replicate the unique qualities that define us," says Anurag Dhingra, senior vice president and general manager of Enterprise Connectivity and Collaboration at Cisco. Therein lies an apparent contradiction: the greater the automation in the workplace, the more human interactions matter.
正在翻译...
"Solving complex problems, sparking innovation --- that happens when people are discussing, debating, and collaborating in real-time," Dhingra says. "The sheer volume of information that AI generates actually amplifies the need for human oversight and connection."
正在翻译...
Consider the possibilities of real-time human-AI collaboration, some of which are already coming true.
正在翻译...
Manufacturing logistics teams can use AI agents that autonomously gather data from demand forecasting, procurement, and tracking systems to fix supply chain bottlenecks before they happen and reduce shipping delays. Product marketing teams can orchestrate creative generation, testing, and analytics agents to cut campaign launch times. And retailers can use AI to plan where, when, and how to stock products and get them into customers' hands sooner.
正在翻译...
The effects of AI-driven transformation will be felt in all types of workplaces, from university and corporate campuses to health clinics and retail stores, and from factories and contact centers to on-the-go locations like cafes and airports. But these kinds of collaborations need everywhere-all-at-once infrastructure that's up to the task.
正在翻译...
For the full potential of human-AI collaboration to be realized, workplaces need a robust digital foundation. At the heart of this foundation is the network --- what Dhingra describes as "the fundamental nervous system for AI."
正在翻译...
Traditional networks weren't built for the demands AI workloads place on bandwidth and reliability. And as AI applications increase network traffic across campuses, branches, and factories, organizations face ever-increasing complexity in managing traffic and devices.
正在翻译...
"If your network isn't up to snuff --- if it has low capacity or high latency --- frustrating delays, dropped connections, and a fragmented experience await," Dhingra says. "Without this foundation, the human potential amplified by AI would be severely hindered."
正在翻译...
Building an infrastructure that can handle both human collaboration and AI workloads requires specific capabilities tailored to each set of users.
正在翻译...
For human-to-human interactions, networks need ubiquitous, high-quality, and low-latency connectivity. That means reliable Wi-Fi and wired connections everywhere work happens, for clear video and voice calls, and rapid access to cloud apps without annoying and productivity-sapping lag.
正在翻译...
For AI, the demands are even higher. "AI agents do a lot of work and communicate at machine speeds," Dhingra says. "Ultra-low latency is critical for autonomous operation and real-time decision-making."
正在翻译...
Advanced AI in the networks themselves is needed to monitor, diagnose, and even correct issues before they become widespread outages. It's already possible thanks to new network components developed by Cisco, like the company's IT management platform. "We enhance this with AgenticOps, which uses real-time telemetry, automation, and deep domain expertise to drive intelligent actions," Dhingra says. "IT teams stay firmly in control, but they can proactively optimize performance, resolve issues before they impact users, and focus on innovation instead of constant troubleshooting."
正在翻译...
Dhingra believes the world of work is moving toward a truly symbiotic relationship between humans and AI, enabled behind the scenes by intelligent infrastructure.
正在翻译...
"As AI evolves from assistants to autonomous digital workers, the workforce expands beyond just human workers to include digital workers (software agents) and even embodied AI, all communicating and collaborating at machine speed and scale," Dhingra says. "There's a symbiotic relationship here we like to call 'Connected Intelligence' --- the tight collaboration between humans and AI agents, and even AI agents and AI agents working together side-by-side."
正在翻译...
As such, networks will dynamically adapt to the demands of digital workers, optimizing traffic, and predicting and preventing issues to ensure rock-solid connectivity at high speed. Just as the internet, our phones, and cloud computing have transformed how we work, AI is ushering in the next great wave of workplace evolution. The infrastructure supporting it is the critical ingredient for making it happen.
