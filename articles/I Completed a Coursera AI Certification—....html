What Happened After I Completed The Coursera Certificate

www.forbes.com Rachel Wells

www.forbes.com 雷切尔·威尔斯

The Illusion of Security

In the last two years, changes to US SEC rules and new regulations like NIS2 and DORA in the EU have forced cybersecurity to become a board-level priority. But while new regulations place direct responsibility for cyber-readiness on boards --- not just on company executives --- non-executive directors often lack a meaningful yardstick to judge readiness. It's tempting to see large security budgets and impressive dashboards and believe that your organization is secure. However, real-world events repeatedly demonstrate that security does not equal readiness. When a breach hits, even the best-funded businesses can find themselves disturbingly unprepared for the chaos that follows.

过去两年，美国 SEC 规则的变化以及欧盟 NIS2 和 DORA 等新法规迫使网络安全成为董事会层面的优先事项。但是，尽管新法规将网络准备就绪的直接责任交给董事会（而不仅仅是公司高管），但非执行董事往往缺乏有意义的标准来判断准备情况。看到大量的安全预算和令人印象深刻的仪表板并相信您的组织是安全的，这是很诱人的。然而，现实世界的事件反复证明，安全并不等于准备就绪。当发生漏洞时，即使是资金最充足的企业也会发现自己对随之而来的混乱毫无准备，这令人不安。

盖蒂

The persistent gap between perception and reality is not just a technical issue --- it's a cultural one. Overconfidence thrives in environments where spending is mistaken for resilience, where cyber risk is "just IT's job," and where the absence of recent incidents is taken as proof of preparedness. These mistakes persist because organizations often lack cross-departmental ownership and critical decisions about what to restore first are wrongly left to technical teams, rather than being treated as essential business decisions.

认知与现实之间持续存在的差距不仅仅是一个技术问题，更是一个文化问题。在错误地将支出视为弹性、网络风险“只是 IT 的工作”以及将近期没有发生事件视为做好准备的环境中，过度自信会滋生。这些错误之所以持续存在，是因为组织通常缺乏跨部门所有权，并且关于首先恢复什么的关键决策被错误地留给了技术团队，而不是被视为重要的业务决策。

The Overconfidence Trap

It's common for boards to measure preparedness by the size of their cybersecurity investments. After all, when boards see that they're spending millions of dollars on security from multiple top tier vendors, it's natural to think they're doing all the right things. The problem is that, as the research behind the Data Resilience Maturity Model points out, most organizations have a false sense of confidence about their own preparedness. Specifically, over 30% of surveyed organizations believed they were more resilient than they actually are, while 74% risk serious data loss in the event of a cyber incident.

董事会通常通过网络安全投资的规模来衡量准备情况。毕竟，当董事会看到他们在多个顶级供应商的安全性上花费了数百万美元时，很自然地认为他们正在做所有正确的事情。问题在于，正如数据弹性成熟度模型背后的研究指出的那样，大多数组织对自己的准备情况抱有错误的信心。具体而言，超过 30% 的受访组织认为他们比实际情况更有弹性，而 74% 的组织在发生网络事件时面临严重数据丢失的风险。

Investment does not necessarily equal readiness, either. You must have leaders from multiple domains involved in the process to be truly cyber ready. Many boards still see cybersecurity as the exclusive domain of IT and Security Operations but fail to recognize that true readiness requires every department to take ownership of its role in preparation and response. This problem is exacerbated as organizations adopt generative AI and automated tools. Both attackers and defenders are leveraging AI, making the threat landscape more complex and dynamic.

投资也不一定等于准备就绪。您必须有来自多个领域的领导者参与此过程，才能真正做好网络准备。许多董事会仍然将网络安全视为 IT 和安全运营的专属领域，但没有认识到真正的准备工作需要每个部门在准备和响应中发挥自己的作用。随着组织采用生成式人工智能和自动化工具，这个问题变得更加严重。攻击者和防御者都在利用人工智能，使威胁形势变得更加复杂和动态。

It's not enough to have an IT team that has the latest security tools deployed if you haven't done a business impact analysis to understand where your data is and what your organization needs to do to get back up and running first. Cyber incidents touch every facet of the organization, from legal and communications to operations and business leadership. When these functions are not aligned, organizations build silos instead of cohesive strategies, leaving dangerous gaps that only become visible during a crisis.

如果您没有进行业务影响分析来了解您的数据在哪里以及您的组织需要做什么才能首先恢复和运行，那么仅仅拥有部署了最新安全工具的 IT 团队是不够的。网络事件涉及组织的各个方面，从法律和通信到运营和业务领导力。当这些职能不一致时，组织就会建立孤岛而不是有凝聚力的战略，从而留下只有在危机期间才会显现出来的危险差距。

Revealing Gaps Through Simulation

Fortunately, there is a proven way to find gaps and fix them prior to an actual disaster: tabletop exercises and resilience workshops. Tabletop exercises often reveal breakdowns in the chain of command for handling incidents when they take place. They can reveal technical shortcomings and expose problems like command chain breakdowns, missing contact lists, and unclear roles. An example: one Fortune 100 company with widely distributed locations and data types took one year after a tabletop drill to decide under what circumstances and for what data they would pay a threat actor's ransom. Obviously, this is not a decision that should be left until crisis strikes.

幸运的是，有一种行之有效的方法可以在实际灾难发生之前找到并修复它们：桌面练习和复原力研讨会。桌面演习通常会揭示事件发生时处理指挥链的故障。它们可以揭示技术缺陷并暴露命令链故障、联系人列表缺失和角色不明确等问题。举个例子：一家位置和数据类型分布广泛的财富 100 强公司在进行桌面演习后花了一年时间才决定在什么情况下以及针对哪些数据向威胁行为者支付赎金。显然，这不应该等到危机爆发才做出决定。

In addition to advanced decision-making, effective crisis communication is everything during an incident. Poor discipline here can exacerbate the damage of a breach, as internal confusion spills over into public scrutiny. For example, there have been incidents where a user photographed a ransomware note during an outage and posted it on social media. Actions like this inform vendors, customers, and investors of a potential incident, which forces your media and corporate communications teams to follow the news rather than lead it.

事件发生期间，除了先进的决策之外，有效的危机沟通也至关重要。纪律不严可能会加剧违规行为的损害，因为内部混乱会蔓延到公众的监督中。例如，曾发生过用户在中断期间拍摄勒索软件记录并将其发布到社交媒体上的事件。此类行为会告知供应商、客户和投资者潜在的事件，这迫使您的媒体和企业传播团队关注新闻而不是主导新闻。

Teams may operate in silos, which can also create regulatory and reputational risks before the board is even fully aware of the incident. AI technologies add another layer of complexity, requiring legal, compliance, and communications teams to be involved in policymaking, governance, and crisis communications. Responsible AI use and governance are quickly becoming board-level concerns. Regular, realistic readiness exercises should involve all key stakeholders, including executives, legal, and communications. These workshops are time and time again shown to be essential to building the muscle memory needed to respond effectively under pressure.

团队可能会各自为政，这甚至可能在董事会完全意识到事件发生之前就造成监管和声誉风险。人工智能技术又增加了一层复杂性，要​​求法律、合规和沟通团队参与政策制定、治理和危机沟通。负责任的人工智能使用和治理正迅速成为董事会层面的关注点。定期、现实的准备演习应让所有主要利益相关者参与，包括高管、法律和沟通人员。这些研讨会一次又一次地被证明对于建立在压力下有效反应所需的肌肉记忆至关重要。

Beyond Metrics: Practicing Readiness

While AI-driven dashboards can provide real-time insights, they can also present new risks if organizations become over-reliant on automated alerts without practicing how to respond to novel, AI-powered attacks. While these indicators have value, they are no substitute for real-world readiness. The unpredictable nature of cyber incidents means that no checklist or SLA can guarantee resilience. Instead, organizations must build muscle memory through hands-on drills, repeated practice, and continuous learning. And to return to boards' regulatory exposure, regular oversight of incident response drills is in fact mandated in both the EU's NIS2 directive and in recent rule updates by the NY Department of Financial Services.

虽然人工智能驱动的仪表板可以提供实时见解，但如果组织过度依赖自动警报而不练习如何应对新型人工智能攻击，它们也可能带来新的风险。虽然这些指标有价值，但它们不能替代现实世界的准备情况。网络事件的不可预测性意味着没有清单或 SLA 可以保证弹性。相反，组织必须通过动手练习、重复练习和持续学习来建立肌肉记忆。回到董事会的监管风险，欧盟的 NIS2 指令和纽约金融服务部最近的规则更新实际上都强制要求对事件响应演习进行定期监督。

Board members and leadership in general have a critical role to play in the implementation of these workshops. By creating a culture of proactive readiness instead of assumed compliance, they can help foster an environment where this communication becomes a pillar of their company. Ensuring a strong, and effective response to disaster is a crucial part of any C-suite member's job.

董事会成员和领导层在这些研讨会的实施中发挥着关键作用。通过创造一种积极主动的准备文化，而不是假设的合规文化，他们可以帮助营造一种环境，使这种沟通成为公司的支柱。确保对灾难做出强有力、有效的响应是任何最高管理层成员工作的重要组成部分。

After all, leadership is tested most visibly during a crisis. When senior leaders are unclear, absent, or unpracticed, trust erodes quickly and organizational response fragments. Effective crisis management demands that executives remain calm, clear, and visible throughout an incident. Boards and the senior leadership team need to be prepared to engage all relevant teams, actively rehearse scenarios where key personnel are unavailable, force others to step into decision-making roles, and ensure that leadership continuity is never in doubt. Unfortunately, this is usually the kind of thing that you only understand if you've gone through very realistic ransomware incident response simulations or if you've been unfortunate enough to go through an event.

毕竟，领导力在危机期间受到的考验最为明显。当高层领导不清楚、缺席或缺乏实践时，信任就会迅速削弱，组织反应也会支离破碎。有效的危机管理要求高管在整个事件过程中保持冷静、清晰和可见。董事会和高级领导团队需要准备好让所有相关团队参与进来，积极演练关键人员无法上场的场景，迫使其他人进入决策角色，并确保领导力的连续性不容置疑。不幸的是，通常只有当您经历过非常现实的勒索软件事件响应模拟或者您不幸经历过某个事件时，您才能理解这种情况。

Redefining Board-Level Cyber Leadership

Many directors fall for the illusion of safety instead of fully comprehending the reality of their situation. To close this readiness gap, boards must move beyond rubber-stamping technology expenditure and take true ownership of organizational resilience. This involves demanding regular simulations, insisting on clear crisis communication protocols, and making readiness a standing agenda item at the highest level. Cyber leadership today is not about asking, "Are we secure?" but "Are we ready?" The difference is profound.

许多董事陷入了安全的幻觉，而不是充分理解自己处境的现实。为了缩小这种准备差距，董事会必须超越橡皮图章技术支出，真正掌握组织弹性。这包括要求定期进行模拟、坚持明确的危机沟通协议以及将准备工作作为最高级别的常设议程项目。今天的网络领导力不是要问“我们安全吗？”但“我们准备好了吗？”差异是深远的。

Boards should leverage updated frameworks that place governance at the center of cybersecurity oversight. They must stay engaged with developments in AI, regulatory requirements, and emerging threats to ensure that oversight and accountability extend beyond the technical sphere to the entire organization. We see the best results when organizations treat every incident or exercise as a learning opportunity and actually follow up with changes. It's about building a culture where everyone expects to learn and improve, not just checking a box.

董事会应利用更新的框架，将治理置于网络安全监督的中心。他们必须持续关注人工智能的发展、监管要求和新出现的威胁，以确保监督和责任从技术领域延伸到整个组织。当组织将每一次事件或练习视为学习机会并实际跟进变革时，我们会看到最好的结果。这是为了建立一种每个人都希望学习和进步的文化，而不仅仅是勾选一个框。
