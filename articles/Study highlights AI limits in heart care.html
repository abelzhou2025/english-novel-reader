Study highlights AI limits in heart care

medicalxpress.com Cedars-Sinai Medical Center

Medicalxpress.com 雪松西奈医疗中心

There are limits in applying AI to images of the heart, according to a new study from the Smidt Heart Institute at Cedars-Sinai published in the Journal of the American Society of Echocardiography.

根据美国超声心动图协会杂志上发表的一项来自西达斯-西奈史密斯心脏研究所的新研究，将人工智能应用于心脏图像存在局限性。

[翻译占位 - 翻译占位符]

Investigators trained multiple artificial intelligence models to read images from echocardiograms, a type of ultrasound test that evaluates the structure and function of the heart. Their goal was to determine whether AI could use these images to calculate measurements like inflammation and scarring that are normally obtained through another, more costly test called cardiac magnetic resonance imaging (CMRI). By examining findings from 1,453 patients who had undergone both tests, they found the AI models could not accomplish this task.

研究人员训练了多个人工智能模型来读取超声心动图图像，超声心动图是一种评估心脏结构和功能的超声测试。他们的目标是确定人工智能是否可以使用这些图像来计算炎症和疤痕等测量结果，这些测量结果通常通过另一种成本更高的测试（称为心脏磁共振成像（CMRI））获得。通过检查 1,453 名接受过两项测试的患者的结果，他们发现人工智能模型无法完成这项任务。

[翻译占位 - 翻译占位符]

"As compared to echocardiograms, cardiac MRI machines are expensive and not available for many patients, especially those in rural areas, so we had hoped that AI could reduce the need for it," said Alan Kwan, MD, assistant professor in the Department of Cardiology in the Smidt Heart Institute at Cedars-Sinai and co-senior author of the study. "Our results showed the limited powers of AI in this area."

“与超声心动图相比，心脏 MRI 机器价格昂贵，许多患者无法使用，尤其是农村地区的患者，因此我们希望人工智能能够减少对心脏 MRI 的需求，”该研究的共同高级作者、西达斯-西奈 Smidt 心脏研究所心脏病学系助理教授 Alan Kwan 医学博士说。“我们的结果表明人工智能在这一领域的能力有限。”
