The commoditization of "AI-first" marketing

正在翻译标题...
Scroll your LinkedIn feed or scan your latest launch calendar. You will notice something odd: Virtually every value proposition uses a similar phrase: "AI-native," "Harnessing the power of AI," or "Revolutionizing \[insert industry here\] with generative AI." We are witnessing a convergence of messaging: distinct product strategies eroded by a rush to claim the same technical territory.
正在翻译...
It feels like marketers got the same memo: Add AI to everything. But in the rush to slap an AI sticker on every product, I have to ask, have we confused the technology for the value proposition?
正在翻译...
This homogeneity is a symptom of strategic anxiety. Boardrooms demand an "AI roadmap," creating a top-down pressure cooker. VCs disproportionately fund the .AI domain. And the fear of being labeled a legacy incumbent is palpable.
正在翻译...
This creates a dangerous cycle. Product teams, under pressure to innovate, start "AI-washing" existing features or bolting on generative models to legacy stacks. The result is a solution looking for a problem. This is handed to marketing with the directive: Make this sound revolutionary.
正在翻译...
We are flooding the market with technical specifications rather than solving business problems. We are selling the chrome-plated oven, while the customer wants a pizza.
正在翻译...
AI is arguably the most significant shift of our lifetime. However, we are mistaking adoption for differentiation. Having a large language model (LLM) in your stack is no longer innovation; it is table stakes.
正在翻译...
Fundamental marketing rules and mandates haven't changed. The medium has changed. Your customer has a problem to solve, and it is not a lack of artificial intelligence in their life.
正在翻译...
Customers are not thinking "If only I could infuse synergistic LLMs into my workflow!" Instead, they're thinking: "Why is this report taking six hours to build?" "Why is our customer support so slow?" and "Why is this expensive? Is there a more efficient way?"
正在翻译...
AI is a game-changing architecture to help answer those questions. But it is the *architecture, not the *value proposition*. Marketing the architecture is like a restaurant bragging about its convection oven. I don't care about your oven. I care about the pizza. Is it delicious? Will it arrive on time?
正在翻译...
We are witnessing a regression in value engineering. We are selling technical capabilities rather than business outcomes. In the enterprise specifically, buyers do not buy novelty; they buy risk mitigation and efficiency gains.
正在翻译...
The era's winners won't be the companies with the most "AI-powered" bullet points. The winners will be marketers who can masterfully translate the opaque technical capabilities into clear, predictable business outcomes.
正在翻译...
The gap is a lack of imagination in how we apply and position these capabilities. We're stuck in a loop of describing the ingredients instead of the meal.
正在翻译...
Bad marketing: "Our website has an AI-powered chat assistant!" So what? Is it going to understand my query, or is it just another automated dead-end?
正在翻译...
Good marketing: "Ask our assistant to find three-bedroom homes near the best schools, and it will also tell you about nearby parks and grocery stores." It saves me two hours of research? You have my attention.
正在翻译...
You've mastered selling the pizza. You're ahead of 80% of the market. But competitors are catching on. Soon, selling benefits will be the baseline.
正在翻译...
The true differentiator isn't just selling a pizza; it's selling the perfect pizza, custom-made with the toppings they didn't know to ask for. Effective marketers will become masters of context, understanding that the same AI feature represents a completely different value proposition to different customers.
正在翻译...
Generic AI marketing: "Integrate location data into your AI models to improve accuracy." It describes the data, not the value.
正在翻译...
Hyper-specific marketing for a proptech innovator: "Don't force homebuyers to filter by location. Use geospatial AI to let them ask, 'Find me a two-bedroom condo within a 15-minute walk of a dog park and a vegan cafe.' You aren't just building a map view; you are building a lifestyle concierge that understands intent." This sells user engagement.
正在翻译...
Hyper-specific marketing for a logistics director:"Eliminate the 'Where is my shipment?' anxiety. Connect your customer service bot to predictive geospatial models so it doesn't just say 'in transit,' but explains, 'Your shipment is delayed by 20 minutes due to unexpected port congestion." This sells operational resilience and trust.
正在翻译...
It is the exact same technical capability. But the delivered solution is completely different because the user's pain point is unique.
正在翻译...
This reinforces the technical product marketer's fundamental responsibility: We are architects of value.
正在翻译...
We operate at the intersection of technical feasibility and business strategy. It is not enough to know the customer; we must reverse-engineer their business models to understand where AI actually drives efficiency. We need to map technical capabilities directly to economic outcomes, understanding their legacy stacks and the specific bottlenecks in their workflows.
正在翻译...
This means your work isn't writing about "the power of AI." It is rigorous value discovery. It requires analyzing support tickets to find friction points and breaking down customer journeys to identify net-new value that can only be unlocked by AI.
正在翻译...
A new model drops every week. Businesses don't need another "AI-powered" tool; they need a strategic guide to help them make the right choice. This requires the discipline to pinpoint exactly where in the user journey AI serves as a force multiplier.
正在翻译...
Crucially, it requires the technical fluency to sometimes tell engineering leadership: "For this specific use case, a generative model is engineering overkill."
正在翻译...
So let's take a deep breath. Marketers falling for the hype will find themselves selling empty features. Those who learn to sell the pizza will do well. But those who stay grounded, who obsess over their customer's problems, and who master the art of selling not just a solution, but the right solution, are the ones who will build the next generation of indispensable brands. Not because they were "AI-native," but because they were, and always will be, customer-obsessed.
