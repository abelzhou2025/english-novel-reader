AI's Dial-Up Era

AI的拨号时代

Nowfal

诺法尔

Oct 17, 2025

2025 年 10 月 17 日

Share

分享

It is 1995.

现在是 1995 年。

Your computer modem screeches as it tries to connect to something called the internet. Maybe it works. Maybe you try again.

当您的计算机调制解调器尝试连接到互联网时会发出刺耳的声音。也许它有效。也许你再试一次。

For the first time in history, you can exchange letters with someone across the world in seconds. Only 2000-something websites exist1, so you could theoretically visit them all over a weekend. Most websites are just text on gray backgrounds with the occasional pixelated image2. Loading times are brutal. A single image takes a minute, a 1-minute video could take hours. Most people do not trust putting their credit cards online. The advice everyone gives: don’t trust strangers on the internet.

历史上第一次，您可以在几秒钟内与世界各地的某人交换信件。目前仅存在 2000 个左右的网站1，因此理论上您可以在一个周末访问所有这些网站。大多数网站只是灰色背景上的文本，偶尔还有像素化图像2。加载时间非常残酷。一张图片需要一分钟，一个一分钟的视频可能需要几个小时。大多数人不信任将信用卡放到网上。每个人都给出的建议：不要相信互联网上的陌生人。

People split into two camps very soon.

人们很快就分成了两个阵营。

Optimists predict grand transformations. Some believe digital commerce will overtake physical retail within years. Others insist we’ll wander around in virtual reality worlds.

乐观者预测巨大的转变。一些人认为，数字商务将在几年内超越实体零售。其他人坚持认为我们将在虚拟现实世界中徘徊。

“I expect that within the next five years more than one in ten people will wear head-mounted computer displays while traveling in buses, trains, and planes.” - Nicholas Negroponte, MIT Professor, 1993

“我预计在未来五年内，超过十分之一的人将在乘坐公共汽车、火车和飞机时佩戴头戴式电脑显示器。”- 尼古拉斯·尼葛洛庞帝，麻省理工学院教授，​​1993 年

Pessimists call the internet a fad and a bubble.

悲观主义者称互联网是一种时尚和泡沫。

If you told the average person in 1995 that within 25 years, we’d consume news from strangers on social media over newspapers, watch shows on-demand in place of cable TV, find romantic partners through apps more than through friends, and flip “don’t trust strangers on the internet” so completely that we’d let internet strangers pick us up in their personal vehicles and sleep in their spare bedrooms, most people would find that hard to believe.

如果你在 1995 年告诉普通人，在 25 年内，我们会在社交媒体上而不是报纸上阅读陌生人的新闻，观看点播节目而不是有线电视，通过应用程序而不是通过朋友寻找浪漫伴侣，并完全翻转“不要相信互联网上的陌生人”，以至于我们会让网络陌生人用他们的私人车辆来接我们并睡在他们的空房间里，大多数人会觉得难以置信。

We’re in 1995 again. This time with Artificial Intelligence.

我们又回到了1995年。这次是人工智能。

And both sides of today’s debate are making similar mistakes.

今天辩论的双方都犯了类似的错误。

One side warns that AI will eliminate entire professions and cause mass unemployment within a couple of years. The other claims that AI will create more jobs than it destroys. One camp dismisses AI as overhyped vaporware destined for a bubble burst, while the other predicts it will automate every knowledge task and reshape civilization within the decade.

一方面警告说，人工智能将在几年内消除整个职业并导致大规模失业。另一种观点则声称人工智能创造的就业机会将多于其破坏的就业机会。一个阵营将人工智能视为过度炒作的汽件，注定会导致泡沫破裂，而另一阵营则预测人工智能将在十年内实现所有知识任务的自动化并重塑文明。

Both are part right and part wrong.

两者都部分正确，部分错误。

The Employment Paradox: Why Automation’s Impact Depends On The Industry

Geoffrey Hinton, who some call the Father of AI, warned in 2016 that AI would trigger mass unemployment. “People should stop training radiologists now,” he declared, certain that AI would replace them within years.

被一些人称为人工智能之父的 Geoffrey Hinton 在 2016 年警告说，人工智能将引发大规模失业。他宣称：“人们现在应该停止培训放射科医生。”他确信人工智能将在几年内取代他们。

Yet as Deena Mousa, a researcher, shows in “The Algorithm Will See You Now,” AI hasn’t replaced radiologists, despite predictions. It is thriving.

然而，正如研究人员迪娜·穆萨 (Deena Mousa) 在《算法即将见到你》中所表明的那样，尽管有预测，人工智能并没有取代放射科医生。它正在蓬勃发展。

In 2025, American diagnostic radiology residency programs offered a record 1,208 positions across all radiology specialties, a four percent increase from 2024, and the field’s vacancy rates are at all-time highs. In 2025, radiology was the second-highest-paid medical specialty in the country, with an average income of $520,000, over 48 percent higher than the average salary in 2015.

2025 年，美国诊断放射学住院医师项目在所有放射学专业中提供了创纪录的 1,208 个职位，比 2024 年增加了 4%，该领域的空缺率创历史新高。2025年，放射科是全国收入第二高的医学专业，平均收入为52万美元，比2015年的平均工资高出48%以上。

Mousa identifies a few factors for why the prediction failed - real-world complexity, the job involves more than image recognition, and regulatory/insurance hurdles. Most critical she points is Jevons Paradox, which is the economic principle that a technological improvement in resource efficiency leads to an increase in the total consumption of that resource, rather than a decrease. Her argument is that as AI makes radiologists more productive, better diagnostics and faster turnaround at lower costs mean more people get scans. So employment doesn’t decrease. It increases.

穆萨指出了预测失败的几个因素：现实世界的复杂性、这项工作涉及的不仅仅是图像识别以及监管/保险障碍。她指出的最关键的是杰文斯悖论，这是一个经济原理，即资源效率的技术改进会导致该资源的总消耗增加，而不是减少。她的论点是，人工智能使放射科医生的工作效率更高，更好的诊断和更快的周转以及更低的成本意味着更多的人接受扫描。所以就业并没有减少。它增加了。

This is also the Tech world’s consensus. Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella agrees, as does Box CEO Aaron Levie, who suggests:

这也是科技界的共识。微软首席执行官萨蒂亚·纳德拉 (Satya Nadella) 表示同意，Box 首席执行官亚伦·莱维 (Aaron Levie) 也表示同意，他建议：

“The least understood yet most important concept in the world is Jevons Paradox. When we make a technology more efficient, demand goes well beyond the original level. AI is the perfect example of this—almost anything that AI is applied to will see more demand, not less.”

“世界上最不被理解但最重要的概念是杰文斯悖论。当我们使一项技术更加高效时，需求将远远超出原始水平。人工智能就是一个完美的例子——几乎任何应用人工智能的东西都会看到更多的需求，而不是更少。”

They’re only half right.

他们只说对了一半。

First, as Andrej Karpathy, the computer scientist who coined the term vibe coding, points out, radiology is not the right job to look for initial job displacements.

首先，正如创造“振动编码”一词的计算机科学家安德烈·卡帕蒂（Andrej Karpathy）指出的那样，放射科并不是寻找最初工作替代的合适工作。

“Radiology is too multi-faceted, too high risk, too regulated. When looking for jobs that will change a lot due to AI on shorter time scales, I’d look in other places - jobs that look like repetition of one rote task, each task being relatively independent, closed (not requiring too much context), short (in time), forgiving (the cost of mistake is low), and of course automatable giving current (and digital) capability. Even then, I’d expect to see AI adopted as a tool at first, where jobs change and refactor (e.g. more monitoring or supervising than manual doing, etc).”

“放射学涉及面太广、风险太高、监管太严。当寻找在较短的时间尺度上因人工智能而发生很大变化的工作时，我会寻找其他地方——看起来像是重复一项死记硬背的任务的工作，每项任务相对独立、封闭（不需要太多背景）、短（时间）、宽容（错误成本低），当然还有可自动化的提供当前（和数字）能力。即便如此，我希望看到人工智能被采用为首先是工具，工作发生变化和重构（例如，比手工操作更多的监控或监督等）。”

Second, the tech consensus that we will see increased employment actually depends on the industry. Specifically, how much unfulfilled demand can be unlocked in that industry, and whether this unfulfilled demand growth outpaces continued automation and productivity improvement.

其次，我们将看到就业增加的技术共识实际上取决于行业。具体来说，该行业可以释放多少未满足的需求，以及这种未满足的需求增长是否超过了自动化和生产率的持续提高。

To understand this better, look at what actually happened in three industries over a 200-year period from 1800 to 2000. In the paper Automation and jobs: when technology boosts employment, James Bessen, an economist, shows the employment, productivity, and demand data for textile, iron & steel, and motor vehicle industries.

为了更好地理解这一点，我们可以看看从 1800 年到 2000 年 200 年间三个行业实际发生的情况。经济学家 James Bessen 在《自动化与就业：当技术促进就业》一文中展示了纺织、钢铁和汽车行业的就业、生产率和需求数据。

After automation, both textile and iron/steel workers saw employment increase for nearly a century before experiencing a steep decline. Vehicle manufacturing, by contrast, holds steady and hasn’t seen the same steep decline yet.

自动化之后，纺织和钢铁工人的就业增长了近一个世纪，然后才经历了急剧下降。相比之下，汽车制造保持稳定，尚未出现同样的急剧下降。

要回答为什么这两个行业出现大幅下降而汽车制造业却没有，首先看看这三个行业工人的生产率：

What the graphs show is a consistent pattern (note: the productivity and demand graphs are logarithmic, meaning productivity and demand grew exponentially). Early on, a service or product is expensive because many workers are needed to produce it. Most people can’t afford it or use them sparingly. For example, in the early 1800s, most people could only afford a pair of pants or shirt. Then automation makes workers dramatically more productive. A textile worker in 1900 could produce fifty times more than one in 1800. This productivity explosion crashes prices, which creates massive new demand. Suddenly everyone can afford multiple outfits instead of just one or two. Employment and productivity both surge (note: employment growth masks internal segment displacement and wage changes. See footnote3)

这些图表显示的是一致的模式（注意：生产力和需求图表是对数的，这意味着生产力和需求呈指数增长）。早期，一项服务或产品是昂贵的，因为需要许多工人来生产它。大多数人买不起或很少使用它们。例如，在 1800 年代初期，大多数人只能买得起一条裤子或衬衫。然后，自动化可以显着提高工人的工作效率。1900 年一名纺织工人的产量是 1800 年的 50 倍。生产率的爆炸性下降导致价格暴跌，从而创造了大量的新需求。突然之间，每个人都可以买得起多套衣服，而不仅仅是一两件。就业和生产率双双飙升（注：就业增长掩盖了内部部门的流动和工资变化。见脚注3）

Once demand saturates, employment doesn’t further increase but holds steady at peak demand. But as automation continues and workers keep getting more productive, employment starts to decline. In textiles, mechanization enabled massive output growth but ultimately displaced workers once consumption plateaued while automation and productivity continued climbing. We probably don’t need infinite clothing. Similarly, patients will likely never need a million radiology reports, no matter how cheap they become and so radiologists will eventually hit a ceiling. We don’t need infinite food, clothing, tax returns, and so on.

一旦需求饱和，就业就不会进一步增加，而是在需求高峰时保持稳定。但随着自动化的继续发展和工人生产力的不断提高，就业率开始下降。在纺织业，机械化实现了产量的大幅增长，但一旦消费趋于稳定，而自动化和生产率持续攀升，最终就会导致工人失业。我们可能不需要无限的衣服。同样，患者可能永远不需要一百万份放射学报告，无论它们变得多么便宜，因此放射科医生最终将达到上限。我们不需要无限的食物、衣服、纳税申报单等等。

Motor vehicles, in Bessen’s graphs, tell a different story because demand remains far from saturated. Most people globally still don’t own cars. Automation hasn’t completely conquered manufacturing either (Tesla’s retreat from full manufacturing automation proves the current technical limits). When both demand and automation potential remain high, employment can sustain or even grow despite productivity gains.

在贝森的图表中，机动车辆讲述了一个不同的故事，因为需求仍未饱和。全球大多数人仍然没有汽车。自动化也还没有完全征服制造业（特斯拉放弃全面制造自动化证明了当前的技术限制）。当需求和自动化潜力都保持在较高水平时，尽管生产率有所提高，就业仍可以维持甚至增长。

Software presents an even more interesting question. How many apps do you need? What about software that generates applications on demand, that creates entire software ecosystems autonomously? Until now, handcrafted software was the constraint. Expensive software engineers and ~~their~~ our labor costs limited what companies could afford to build. Automation changes this equation by making those engineers far more productive. Both consumer and enterprise software markets suggest significant unmet demand because businesses have consistently left projects unbuilt4. They couldn’t justify the development costs or had to allocate limited resources to their top priority projects. I saw this firsthand at Amazon. Thousands of ideas went unfunded not because they lacked business value, but because of the lack of engineering resources to build them. If AI can produce software at a fraction of the cost, that unleashes enormous latent demand. The key question then is if and when that demand will saturate.

软件提出了一个更有趣的问题。您需要多少个应用程序？按需生成应用程序、自主创建整个软件生态系统的软件怎么样？到目前为止，手工制作的软件仍然是制约因素。昂贵的软件工程师和〜他们的〜我们的劳动力成本限制了公司可以负担的建设费用。自动化使这些工程师的工作效率大大提高，从而改变了这一方程式。消费者和企业软件市场都显示出大量未满足的需求，因为企业一直没有建设项目4。他们无法证明开发成本合理，或者不得不将有限的资源分配给最优先的项目。我在亚马逊亲眼目睹了这一点。数以千计的想法没有得到资助，并不是因为它们缺乏商业价值，而是因为缺乏构建它们的工程资源。如果人工智能能够以一小部分成本生产软件，那就会释放巨大的潜在需求。那么关键问题是这种需求是否以及何时会饱和。

The Bubble: Irrational Exuberance Builds the Future

The second debate centers on whether this AI boom is a bubble waiting to burst.

第二场争论的焦点是人工智能的繁荣是否是一个即将破裂的泡沫。

The dotcom boom of the 1990s saw a wave of companies adding “.com” to their name to ride the mania and watch their valuations soar. Companies poured billions into fiber optics and undersea cables - expensive projects only possible because people believed the hype5. All of this eventually burst in spectacular fashion in the dotcom crash in 2000-2001. Infrastructure companies like Cisco briefly became the most valuable in the world only to come tumbling down6. Pets.com served as the poster child of this exuberance raising $82.5 million in its IPO, spending millions on a Super Bowl ad only to collapse nine months later7.

20 世纪 90 年代的互联网热潮见证了一批公司在其名称中添加“.com”，以乘风破浪并见证其估值飙升。公司在光纤和海底电缆上投入了数十亿美元——昂贵的项目只有因为人们相信炒作才可能实现。所有这一切最终在 2000 年至 2001 年的互联网泡沫破灭中以惊人的方式爆发。像思科这样的基础设施公司曾一度成为世界上最有价值的公司，但后来却一落千丈6。Pets.com 是这种繁荣的典型代表，在 IPO 中筹集了 8250 万美元，花费数百万美元制作超级碗广告，但九个月后就倒闭了7。

But the dotcom bubble also got several things right. More importantly, it eventually bought us the physical infrastructure that made YouTube, Netflix, and Facebook possible. Sure, companies like Worldcom, NorthPoint, and Global Crossing making these investments went bankrupt, but they also laid the foundation for the future. Although the crash proved the skeptics right in the short term, it proved the optimists were directionally correct in the long term.

但互联网泡沫也做了一些正确的事情。更重要的是，它最终为我们购买了物理基础设施，使 YouTube、Netflix 和 Facebook 成为可能。当然，世通、NorthPoint 和 Global Crossing 等进行这些投资的公司都破产了，但它们也为未来奠定了基础。尽管这次崩盘在短期内证明了怀疑论者是正确的，但从长远来看，它证明了乐观主义者的方向是正确的。

Today’s AI boom shows similar exuberance. Consider the AI startup founded by former OpenAI executive Mira Murati, which raised $2 billion at a $10 billion valuation, the largest seed round in history8. This despite having no product and declining to reveal what it’s building or how it will generate returns. Several AI wrappers have raised millions in seed funding with little to no moat.

今天的人工智能热潮也表现出类似的繁荣。以 OpenAI 前高管 Mira Murati 创立的人工智能初创公司为例，该公司以 100 亿美元的估值筹集了 20 亿美元，这是历史上最大的种子轮融资8。尽管没有产品并且拒绝透露其正在构建什么或将如何产生回报，但仍然如此。一些人工智能包装商已经筹集了数百万美元的种子资金，但几乎没有护城河。

Yet some investments will outlast the hype and will likely help future AI companies even if this is a bubble. For example, the annual capital expenditures of Hyperscalers9 that have more than doubled since ChatGPT’s release - Microsoft, Google, Meta, and Amazon are collectively spending almost half a trillion dollars on data centers, chips, and compute infrastructure. Regardless of which specific companies survive, this infrastructure being built now will create the foundation for our AI future - from inference capacity to the power generation needed to support it.

然而，一些投资将比炒作更持久，并且可能会帮助未来的人工智能公司，即使这是一个泡沫。例如，自 ChatGPT 发布以来，Hyperscalers9 的年度资本支出增加了一倍多 - 微软、谷歌、Meta 和亚马逊在数据中心、芯片和计算基础设施上总共花费了近 5000 亿美元。无论哪些特定公司能够生存下来，现在正在建设的基础设施都将为我们的人工智能未来奠定基础——从推理能力到支持它所需的发电。

The infrastructure investments may have long-term value, but are we already in bubble territory? Azeem Azhar, a tech analyst and investor, provides an excellent practical framework to answer the AI bubble question. He benchmarks today’s AI boom using five gauges: economic strain (investment as a share of GDP), industry strain (capex to revenue ratios), revenue growth trajectories (doubling time), valuation heat (price-to-earnings multiples), and funding quality (the resilience of capital sources). His analysis shows that AI remains in a demand-led boom rather than a bubble, but if two of the five gauges head into red, we will be in bubble territory.

基础设施投资可能具有长期价值，但我们是否已经处于泡沫领域？科技分析师兼投资者 Azeem Azhar 提供了一个出色的实用框架来回答人工智能泡沫问题。他使用五个指标来衡量当今人工智能的繁荣：经济压力（投资占 GDP 的比重）、行业压力（资本支出与收入之比）、收入增长轨迹（倍增时间）、估值热度（市盈率倍数）和融资质量（资本来源的弹性）。他的分析表明，人工智能仍然处于需求主导的繁荣状态，而不是泡沫，但如果五个指标中的两个变为红色，我们将处于泡沫区域。

The demand is real. After all OpenAI is one of the fastest-growing companies in history10. But that alone doesn’t prevent bubbles. OpenAI will likely be fine given its product-market fit, but many other AI companies face the same unit economics questions that plagued dotcom companies in the 1990s. Pets.com had millions of users too (a then large portion of internet users), but as the tech axiom goes, you can acquire infinite customers and generate infinite revenue if you sell dollars for 85 cents11. So despite the demand, the pattern may be similar to the 1990s. Expect overbuilding. Expect some spectacular failures. But also expect the infrastructure to outlast the hype cycle and enable things we can’t yet imagine.

需求是真实的。毕竟 OpenAI 是历史上增长最快的公司之一10。但仅此并不能防止泡沫。考虑到其产品与市场的契合度，OpenAI 可能会表现良好，但许多其他人工智能公司也面临着 20 世纪 90 年代困扰互联网公司的同样的单位经济学问题。Pets.com 也拥有数百万用户（当时互联网用户的很大一部分），但正如科技公理所说，如果您以 85 美分的价格出售美元，您就可以获得无限的客户并产生无限的收入11。因此，尽管有需求，但模式可能与 20 世纪 90 年代相似。预期过度建设。预计会出现一些引人注目的失败。但也期望基础设施能够超越炒作周期并实现我们尚无法想象的事情。

The Predictably Unpredictable Future

So where does this leave us?

那么这会给我们带来什么影响呢？

We’re early in the AI revolution. We’re at that metaphorical screeching modem phase of the internet era. Just as infrastructure companies poured billions into fiber optics, hyperscalers now pour billions into data centers. Startups add “.ai” to their names like companies once added “.com” as they seek higher valuations. The hype will cycle through both euphoria and despair. Some predictions will look laughably wrong. Some that seem crazy will prove conservative.

我们正处于人工智能革命的早期阶段。我们正处于互联网时代隐喻的尖叫现代阶段。正如基础设施公司向光纤投入数十亿美元一样，超大规模企业现在也向数据中心投入数十亿美元。初创公司在名称中添加“.ai”，就像公司在寻求更高估值时添加“.com”一样。炒作将在欣喜和绝望之间循环。有些预测看起来错误得可笑。有些看似疯狂的做法最终会被证明是保守的。

Different industries will experience different outcomes. Unlike what the Jevons optimists suggest, demand for many things plateaus once human needs are met. Employment outcomes in any industry depend on the magnitude and growth of unmet market demand and whether that demand growth outpaces productivity improvements from automation.

不同的行业会经历不同的结果。与杰文斯乐观主义者所说的不同，一旦人类的需求得到满足，对许多东西的需求就会趋于稳定。任何行业的就业结果都取决于未满足的市场需求的规模和增长，以及需求增长是否超过自动化带来的生产率提高。

Cost reduction will unlock market segments. Aswath Damodaran, a finance professor, (in)famously undervalued Uber assuming it would only capture a portion of the existing taxi market12. He missed that making rides dramatically cheaper would expand the market itself as people took Ubers to destinations they’d never have paid taxi prices to reach. AI will similarly enable products and services currently too expensive to build with human intelligence. A restaurant owner might use AI to create custom supply chain software that with human developers, say at $100,000, would never have been built. A non-profit might deploy AI to contest a legal battle that was previously unaffordable.

成本降低将释放细分市场。金融学教授阿斯瓦斯·达莫达兰 (Aswath Damodaran) 因低估 Uber 的价值而闻名，他认为 Uber 只能占领现有出租车市场的一部分12。他错过了，大幅降低乘车价格会扩大市场本身，因为人们会乘坐优步前往他们从未支付过出租车价格的目的地。人工智能同样将使目前用人类智能构建起来过于昂贵的产品和服务成为可能。餐馆老板可能会使用人工智能来创建定制的供应链软件，而如果使用人类开发人员，比如花费 10 万美元，这是永远无法构建的。非营利组织可能会部署人工智能来应对以前无法承受的法律诉讼。

We can predict change, but we can’t predict the details. No one in 1995 predicted we’d date strangers from the internet, ride in their ubers, or sleep in their airbnbs. Or that a job called influencers would become the most sought-after career among young people. Human creativity generates outcomes we can’t forecast with our current mental models. Expect new domains and industries to emerge. AI has already helped us decode more animal communication in the last five years than in the last fifty. Can we predict what jobs a technology that allows us to have full-blown conversations with them will unlock? A job that doesn’t exist today will likely be the most sought-after job in 2050. We can’t name it because it hasn’t been invented yet.

我们可以预测变化，但无法预测细节。1995 年，没有人预料到我们会通过互联网与陌生人约会，乘坐他们的优步，或者睡在他们的爱彼迎上。或者说，一种被称为影响者的工作将成为年轻人中最抢手的职业。人类创造力产生的结果是我们目前的思维模式无法预测的。预计新的领域和产业将会出现。人工智能在过去五年中已经帮助我们解码了比过去五十年更多的动物通讯。我们能否预测一项允许我们与他们进行全面对话的技术将解锁哪些工作？今天不存在的工作可能会在 2050 年成为最受欢迎的工作。我们无法说出它的名字，因为它还没有被发明出来。

Job categories will transform. Even as the internet made some jobs obsolete, it also transformed others and created new categories. Expect the same with AI. Karpathy ends with a question:

工作类别将会发生变化。尽管互联网让一些工作变得过时，但它也改变了其他工作并创造了新的类别。预计人工智能也是如此。卡帕蒂以一个问题结束：

About 6 months ago, I was also asked to vote if we will have less or more software engineers in 5 years. Exercise left for the reader.

大约 6 个月前，我还被要求投票：5 年后我们会减少还是增加软件工程师。练习留给读者。

The same pattern will play out with software engineers. We’ll see more people doing software engineering work and in a decade or so, what “software engineer” means will have transformed. Consider the restaurant owner from earlier who uses AI to create custom inventory software that is useful only for them. They won’t call themselves a software engineer.

同样的模式也将在软件工程师身上上演。我们将看到更多的人从事软件工程工作，在十年左右的时间里，“软件工程师”的含义将会发生变化。考虑一下早期的餐馆老板，他们使用人工智能创建只对他们有用的自定义库存软件。他们不会称自己为软件工程师。

So just like in 1995, if the AI optimists today say that within 25 years, we’d prefer news from AI over social media influencers, watch AI-generated characters in place of human actors, find romantic partners through AI matchmakers more than through dating apps (or perhaps use AI romantic partners itself), and flip “don’t trust AI” so completely that we’d rely on AI for life-or-death decisions and trust it to raise our children, most people would find that hard to believe. Even with all the intelligence, both natural and artificial, no one can predict with certainty what our AI future will look like. Not the tech CEOs, not the AI researchers, and certainly not some random guy pontificating on the internet. But whether we get the details right or not, our AI future is loading.

因此，就像 1995 年一样，如果今天的人工智能乐观主义者说，在 25 年内，我们更喜欢来自人工智能的新闻而不是社交媒体影响者，观看人工智能生成的角色而不是人类演员，通过人工智能媒人而不是通过约会应用程序寻找浪漫伴侣（或者可能使用人工智能浪漫伴侣本身），并完全翻转“不信任人工智能”，以至于我们依赖人工智能做出生死攸关的决定并相信它来抚养我们的孩子，大多数人会觉得很难相信。即使拥有所有的智能，无论是自然的还是人工的，没有人能够确定地预测我们的人工智能未来会是什么样子。不是科技公司的首席执行官，不是人工智能研究人员，当然也不是一些在互联网上胡言乱语的人。但无论我们的细节是否正确，我们的人工智能未来都在加载。

Historical data from the Industrial Revolution shows that even as aggregate textile employment grew, workers shifted between job types within the industry as some roles became redundant. And correspondingly, some jobs saw wages collapse while others saw increases. For example, domestic hand-loom weavers were displaced by power looms and saw their wages collapse, while self-acting mule spinners (a newly created role) and factory workers saw stable employment and steady compensation growth. Additionally, Britain’s deflationary period (1815-1850) saw food prices fall by half, meaning real purchasing power often rose even when nominal wages declined. Despite all this, the psychological reality was harsh. Even with falling prices, watching your paycheck shrink while neighbors lost jobs and debts grew harder to pay (lower wages but fixed obligations) created real instability regardless of aggregate statistics improvements. Also see Acemoglu & Johnson, 2024.

工业革命的历史数据表明，尽管纺织业就业总量增长，但由于某些角色变得多余，工人在行业内的工作类型之间转移。相应地，一些工作的工资大幅下降，而另一些工作的工资却有所增加。例如，国内手织机织工被动力织机取代，工资大幅下降，而自走锭纺纱工（新设立的岗位）和工厂工人则就业稳定，薪酬稳步增长。此外，在英国的通货紧缩时期（1815-1850），食品价格下降了一半，这意味着即使名义工资下降，实际购买力也往往会上升。尽管如此，心理现实还是严酷的。即使物价下跌，无论总体统计数据如何改善，看着自己的薪水缩水，邻居失业，债务变得更难偿还（工资降低但义务固定），都会造成真正的不稳定。另请参阅 Acemoglu & Johnson，2024。
