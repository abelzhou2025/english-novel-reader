5 Reasons Why Expert Agents Are Better than Generic AI

正在翻译标题...
For business leaders who are spearheading AI initiatives, reality hasn't always lived up to their high expectations for the technology. Early experiments with generative AI and large language models have been interesting, but not very profitable. However, shifting away from generic AI to expert autonomous agents could lead to better business outcomes.
正在翻译...
Companies are undeniably optimistic about the potential for AI to transform their businesses. A NewtonX survey sponsored by Teradata found that 99% have already implemented some form of AI.
正在翻译...
Customer experience (CX) agents are a particularly attractive use case, with 85% of those polled saying they are considering, evaluating or currently using agentic AI for this purpose. In addition, the survey found that 81% of respondents were "very confident" or "extremely confident" that autonomous agents could improve CX. In addition, more than half of companies surveyed anticipated generating at least $1 million in revenue or cost savings from AI for CX initiatives, with 36% expecting over $2.5 million in benefits.
正在翻译...
In particular, legal/compliance teams (75%) and IT/security (75%) are expressing reservations, with a majority of leadership (55%) also feeling some apprehension. Only 2.2% of those surveyed said no one in their organization was flagging potential risks.
正在翻译...
Those doubts are well-founded. MIT's often-cited report on The GenAI Divide found that "Despite $30--40 billion in enterprise investment into GenAI, ... 95% of organizations are getting zero return."
正在翻译...
Part of the reason for these poor results may be that, to date, most enterprise AI efforts have centered around general-purpose LLMs and generic agents. These tools have some advantages: They are good at summarizing a broad range of knowledge. They are very flexible and adaptable, and they can help spark creativity.
正在翻译...
However, generic agents have well-publicized issues with accuracy. They can hallucinate data that doesn't actually exist, and they are biased towards telling users what they think the user wants to hear.
正在翻译...
By contrast, a expert agent focuses in on a narrow domain. It answers questions with much more specificity, providing insights that can yield real business results.
正在翻译...
For example, Teradata recently launched a software and services offering called Autonomous Customer Intelligence that features expert agents. One of those agents focuses on customer lifetime value (CLV). Companies have long used AI models to predict CLV, but those predictive tools lack the ability to take actions based on real-time signals. Some organizations have tried using generic agents for this purpose, but general-purpose tools don't understand the context well enough to make accurate recommendations and act on them. It requires enterprise knowledge to transform data into action.
正在翻译...
An expert agent overcomes both sets of limitations. Imagine that a retailer wants to figure out which customers it should target with a holiday promotion. The expert agent could pull the relevant data from a variety of sources and then calculate CLV for all customers. It could then flag the top 10% and automatically send them personalized discounts through the marketing automation system. With Teradata's hybrid, enterprise-grade data foundation and built-in AI governance, these expert agents operate with trust and precision---acting in real time and delivering measurable outcomes.
正在翻译...
Expert agents, like the Teradata CLV agent, have five big advantages over standard LLMs and generic agents:
正在翻译...
1. Business context --- Expert agents come pre-trained with a deep understanding of the domain they were designed for. They understand jargon and constraints without the need for additional training, resulting in more insightful analysis. 2. Integration --- In addition, these tools easily integrate with existing CRMs, databases, business intelligence platforms, and other tools. Instead of providing generic responses, they can tailor their output to specific situations. 3. Precision --- As a result of that deep domain knowledge and integration with actual data, expert agents can be much more precise than a generic tool. They make fewer errors and have far fewer hallucinations. 4. Governance --- More accurate autonomous agents are far easier to oversee. Because of the well-defined use cases, it's far easier to apply guardrails and ethics rules, and audits become simpler as well. 5. ROI --- When it comes to resources, a expert agent has the right data to solve problems faster, driving greater agility while consuming less energy and compute cycles. It speeds up the decision-making process by already having the intelligence needed to drive business goals forward, allowing companies to see a return on their investment more quickly.
正在翻译...
As companies continue to demand more from AI, expert agents are likely to play an increasingly significant role in the future. And Teradata will be leading the way.
